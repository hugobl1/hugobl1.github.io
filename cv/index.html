Work

  • 2022.10 - Present
    PhD Candidate in 3D Computer Vision and Rendering
    Mines Paris – CAOR
    Research on 3D scene reconstruction from images using local Gaussian and RBF-based volumetric representations, with a focus on novel view synthesis and implicit scene representations.
    • Main research contributions include RayGauss (volumetric Gaussian-based ray casting) and RayGaussX (accelerated Gaussian-based ray marching).
    • Developing a global implicit surface model from local RBFs for accurate, BVH-accelerated geometry reconstruction.
    • Daily development in Unix/Linux with C++, Python, CUDA and OptiX; extensive use of PyTorch, OpenCV and git.
  • 2022.04 - 2022.09
    Research Intern
    Mines Paris – CAOR
    Research intern on accelerating NeRF-style volumetric rendering for interactive novel view synthesis, leveraging camera pose optimisation and 3D point cloud data (LiDAR / MVS).
    • Explored strategies to combine NeRF-style rendering with LiDAR / MVS point cloud priors.
    • Worked on camera pose optimisation for interactive novel view synthesis.
